## Ingredients

- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 1 cup of cheese (cheddar, Colby, etc.), shredded
- 1/2 lb broccoli florets, chopped
- 1/2 yellow onion, chopped
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tsp salt, divided
- 1/4 tsp freshly ground black pepper, divided
- 1/2 lb dried orzo pasta
- 2 cups chicken or bone broth
- 1/2 cup milk
- 1 tbsp unsalted butter


## Instructions

1. Heat olive oil in a pot over medium heat. Add the onion, 1/2 teaspoon of salt, and pinch of black pepper. Cook until the onion is translucent. 
2. Add the broccoli, remaining salt & pepper and stir to combine. Cook, until the florets are vibrant green, 3 to 5 minutes. 
3. Add the garlic, orzo, and broth and stir to combine. Bring to a boil then reduce the heat to maintain a very gentle simmer. Continue to cook, stirring every minute or so, until the orzo al dente (about 7 minutes) and most of the liquid is absorbed. 
4. Add milk, stir to combine, and simmer for about 1 minute more. Remove the pot from the heat. Add butter and the shredded cheese and stir. Let sit for 5 minutes for the sauce to thicken before serving.
